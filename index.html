Professional Narrative

I am a microbial genomics expert dedicated to combating Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR) through a One Health framework. My research integrates advanced shotgun metagenomics and high-throughput sequencing to characterize multidrug-resistant pathogens and their transmission across human-animal interfaces.

Currently, I serve as the Chair of the ESCMID Study Group on Epidemiological Markers (ESGEM). My career includes pivotal roles at the University of Oxford, University of Groningen, and University of Bath, alongside leading international collaborations to standardize genomic surveillance.

Education Course (ESCMID)

Role: Co-Coordinator

Coordinated, alongside, Louise Roer (SSI), the development and delivery of the course entitled Tracking plasmids for clinical and public health use.

Global Genomic Surveillance of AMR (NIHR)

Role: Work package lead

Led KLEbSurvey design, a genomic survey to assess the diversity of CRKP in low and middle-income countries, coordinated multi-country data integration, contributed to interpretation and reporting of large-scale AMR trends, and supported capacity building for genomic surveillance partners.

SECRETERS (MSCA-ITN, Horizon 2020)

Role: Work package lead / Supervisor

Developed training materials and supervised early-career researchers on experimental and bioinformatic methods for studying bacterial secretion systems, and contributed to methodological development and project governance.

Genomic Diversity of Mycoplasma pneumoniae (ESCMID)

Role: Principal investigator (ESCMID-funded study)

Designed the study, oversaw sample processing and sequencing workflows, led analyses of genomic diversity and population structure, and translated findings into surveillance recommendations.

One Health Drivers of AMR in Thailand (UKRI)

Role: Co-Investigator / Collaborator

Contributed to study design and sampling strategy across human, animal and environmental sectors, led genomic analyses linking sources and transmission pathways, and supported local capacity strengthening.
